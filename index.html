About

Smart Talents Scout is a web based candidate recommending system which goes along the line of these online platforms, but with additional capabilities. It will ease the workload of a job recruitment company by selecting the best matching candidates for their job requirements. Main goal of this project would be to locate the perfect matching candidates for a given job requirement with the assistance of an efficient candidate ranking. This will lead the job seekers and employers to persuasively and productively fulfil their requirements while conveniently having access his or her most interested jobs and candidates.

Literature Survey

The current worldwide economic crisis implies that organizations today are, dealt with the difficulty of having far less cash-flow to contribute within their Human Resource divisions, while equally having to guarantee, that they are choosing the most qualified and skillful candidates suited for a job opening [1]. For the past few years we have witnessed a marked increment in the number of websites committed to providing a facility for job search, where job seekers with distinctive work experience and skills will retrieve precisely the same output.

According to an online report, in excess of 200 million unique users look for employments online every month. This incredibly extensive and quickly developing interest has tempted software giants such as Google and Facebook to enter this space, which was previously commanded by companies like Indeed, LinkedIn, CareerBuilder and Dice. Google released their "AI powered Jobs Search Engine", "Google For Jobs" which shows postings from most of the popular job search websites mentioned above, While Facebook released "Facebook Jobs" inside their platform. These job search sites, in most cases, do not have the rich range of abilities that employers need to locate the best candidate matched to their own preferences, their capacities, and their interests and goals [2]. In order to conquer the disadvantages in these traditional job portals numerous researches have been done to develop candidate recommending systems. Candidate recommending systems, a particular sub system under a bigger class of recommender systems, have additionally been the subject of various research studies [3].

A few online recruitment sites and HR consultancy and enlistment organizations like Monster.com empower job seekers to create their resume so as to search and apply for desirable jobs, while they enable employers to find suitable representatives they are searching for. Likewise, job career portals like LinkedIn has picked up fame as a professional social networking site such that they are becoming the greatest source for finding suitable candidates and most of the recruitment firms utilize LinkedIn as a primary source of recruitment.

CareerBuilder has developed an in-house skill term extraction system, called SKILL that can retrieve skill keywords from both job seekers resumes and job posts. Despite the fact that such extracted skills are each joined with a certainty score, the scores speak to the probability of each skill, except not the importance of the skill to the job title. Studying the skill relevance to a job title remains a major challenge. Endorsement, for instance, is a component on LinkedIn that incorporates labels contributed by individuals' connections. This feature depends intensely on the nature of human input, and subsequently, may experience the ill effects of blunders because of heterogeneity in users' adequacy [4].

Customized recommender Systems have seen an increase in demand and had been broadly utilized as a part of numerous domains in the most recent decades by the researchers. The jobrecommender system is proposed for applicants who seek job positions and recruitment firms who discover candidates on the Internet by applying the idea of customized recommender systems into the recruitment space. With the examination by researchers, a few employment recommender systems which have a specific application foundation have been executed and generate the satisfactory recommendation results [11].


Research Problem

As the hiring needs of organizations request a few traits separated from their specific abilities rivalry for talent discovery from a restricted pool of applicants turns out to be more intense. One of primary challenges faced by the employers to locate the most proper candidate for a job role is that several attributes of the candidate should be considered before decision making. Even though, much of the search result is in terms of skill matching percentage to the job requirement, professional and academic background adds to the capacity to adequately choose a set of suitable candidates from a vast pool. Thus, narrowing a search remains a difficult task for a machine, provided that there is no policy used to rank the candidates [12].

While traditional searching techniques gives users a fine control over discovering potential candidates for a job role recruitment firms are unable to locate the top ranked or in other words most suitable candidates for a desired job role. As a solution, many job recommending systems such as Indeed and Glassdoor that could beat this disadvantage has been implemented. Thinking about the various needs of users, a job recommender system can be intended for various target users, i.e. job seekers or recruiters. For applicants, the job recommender system empowers them to create their own profile, input their personal information, upload their resume, and to get their favored job role information. For employers, they can post the enlisting job roles and the data of likeness possibility for their posted employments can be given in the job recommender system. A job recommender system produces distinctive user profile and embraces the relating recommendation innovation in light of the attributes of target users with a similar objective of prescribing the fitting employments or candidates to target users [10].

Although recommending systems have been developed for both the above mentioned target users. Most of them have been developed mainly for job seekers while the ones which also serves for employers such as Indeed.com requires them to post their job requirements in a pre-defined or unstructured format. This has become a cumbersome task for the recruiters as they have to advertise their vacancies and adding to that, it does not guarantee that all the requirements for the job has been taken in to consideration.

Large enterprises get quite a few resumes from job applicants each day. In this manner it is required to implement a framework that has the capacity of extracting a few vital useful fields from a resume utilizing a set of NLP techniques. Ideally, an enterprise would like to get rid of compelling its applicants to fill in a predefined layout given that they approached a framework that could extract the required data, from any format of resume, both structured and unstructured. ResuMatcher is a case for this as it doesn't limit the candidate resumes to a particular template. In any case, Experimental outcomes completed on a substantial number of resumes demonstrate that a proposed framework can deal with an extensive assortment of resumes in various document formats with an exactness of 91 % and a review of 88% which demonstrates that we can't ensure that this strategy is sufficiently compelling to extract all the required data [13]. Therefore we propose a standard structured resume for the candidates as it would push the undertaking to easily and rapidly search for the correct candidate.

Research Objectives


Main Objectives
To implement a simple cost effective candidate recommendation system that asses resumes submitted through a website, and provides a ranking based on candidate profile, skills and experience, which will be useful for the employers to select top candidates of their requirement form a vast pool of candidates.

Specific Objectives
To extract data from structured resumes, reformulate the information and classify them.
To extract the job requirements to store them and ranking the candidate according to their skills againts the job requirements.
To rank the candidate skill level by conducting an in-site examination according to their skills listed in their resume
To implement a recommendation mechanism to find the top ranked candidates as per employer requirement.
